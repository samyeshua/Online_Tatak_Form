# Online_Tatak_Form

Smart Attendance System (SAS) is a web-based and mobile-responsive attendance verification
system designed to modernize how university-wide events are managed. Currently, large-scale activities
such as intramurals, general orientations, and institutional programs involving over a thousand students
rely on a manual "stamp-form" system. This traditional process is frequently characterized by inefficiency
and inaccuracies, including the risk of lost physical forms, duplicate stamping, and significant delays in
consolidating attendance reports for organizations. By transitioning to a digital framework, the SAS
automates attendance monitoring and ensures that all records are centralized, accurate, and easily
accessible to authorized stakeholders.
The SAS provides distinct, role-specific dashboards for Admins, Officers, and Students, allowing
for a streamlined user experience tailored to the needs of each group. Admins manage the overall system
parameters and organization accounts, Officers oversee specific event windows and monitor real-time
counts, while Students can easily log in to confirm their attendance and download personal certificates.
A primary focus of the system is to establish a high level of reliability and auditability by
implementing venue-based confirmation. To combat the issue of proxy attendance where students might
attempt to scan in for their peers, the SAS requires a real-time photo submission or "selfie" during the
transaction process. This photo is attached to the record for immediate verification by organization
officers, who have the authority to void any entries that appear to be fraudulent, such as a "picture of a
picture". Furthermore, attendance can only be confirmed within authorized timeframes and through
specific validation mechanisms like QR codes, ensuring students are physically present at the venue.
